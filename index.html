TIMELINE

1913 – Robert Adler is born in Vienna, Austria. 1937 – Earns a Ph.D. in physics from the University of Vienna. 1939 – Emigrates to the United States due to the rise of Nazi Germany. 1941 – Joins Zenith Electronics (formerly Zenith Radio Corporation) as an engineer. 1950s – Works on television technology, including remote controls and signal transmission. 1956 – Co-invents the first practical wireless TV remote control, the "Zenith Space Command," using ultrasonic technology. 1960s-1970s – Continues research on electronics, including surface acoustic wave (SAW) technology. 1980 – Becomes Vice President and Director of Research at Zenith Electronics. 1982 – Receives an Emmy Award for his contributions to television remote control technology. 2007 – Passes away at the age of 93 in Boise, Idaho.
